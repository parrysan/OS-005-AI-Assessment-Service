Opportunity & Model

What SMBs now want, and the service that answers.

For three years, AI-for-SMB was mostly noise. That changed in 2025: investment in AI among small firms jumped 57%, and most adopters reported revenue gains within twelve months.

What these owners want isn't a strategy deck — it's someone who knows what to do on Monday morning. The market is large, the competition is shallow, and the service that wins is the one that makes the first move feel obvious.

i.

A 20-minute discovery call

A voice agent — call her Annie — interviews the business owner. No Zoom shadowing, no consultant time. A structured conversation captures real operational pain in the owner's own words.
ii.

AI analysis of the transcript

The transcript is fed to Claude, which surfaces underlying pain points and matches them to off-the-shelf tools that already solve them. No bespoke build, no vapourware.
iii.

A "Quick Win" report

A written report: an Effort × Impact matrix, the two or three highest-leverage moves, and a 4-day implementation plan the owner could in principle run themselves.
iv.

Implementation, on request

Most owners won't run it themselves. The report is the door-opener; the implementation work is where engagement value compounds. This is where the model stops looking like consulting.
